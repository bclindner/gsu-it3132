The Scrum Team

Scrum doesn't have a distinct hierarchy like other frameworks. No member of a team is above another, or vice versa. Instead, team members assume one of three distinct roles which defines the scope of their work - a Development Team member, a Product Owner, or a Scrum Master.

Who is the Scrum Master?

The Scrum Master's role is to ensure that Scrum is understood and perfoemd properly, ensuring the rest of the team follows Scrum methodology and all practices and rules that come with it.

Who is the Product Owner?

The Product Owner is the one who takes responsibility for a product, and in maximizing its value. The way this is enacted varies by organization.
One key thing that the Product Owner must manage is the Product Backlog. They must ensure that the backlog is well-kept and well-written, and ordered to improve product quality.

Who is a Development Team Member?

The larger portion of a Scrum team will be Development Team members. They are the proverbial worker bees of the Sprint - they write code, design graphics and content and put the product together as a team. Members self-assign tasks and perform them as needed during Sprints to develop the working product.

Do Development Team members need to all know how to code, write, design, etc.?

Absolutely not. Development Team members can be dedicated coders or graphic designers, and only choose tasks which involve their skills. However, multitalented members may have a number of strengths, allowing them to take on different tasks.
