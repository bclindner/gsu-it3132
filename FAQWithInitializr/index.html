Scrum Events

In Scrum, certain events are planned to minimize the need for meetings and to keep things regular. Everything is time-boxed as well, which ensures no events take too much time, ensuring efficiency. Two major events that will be discussed on this page are Sprints and the Daily Scrum.

What exactly is a Sprint?

A sprint is a time-boxed window of development that generates a usable, potentially shippable product. Sprints are performed consecutively, usually every 30 days. Sprints are only cancelled in rare cases, and the goals of a Sprint do not change during development, though the scope may change if conditions permit.

What is a Daily Scrum?

A Daily Scrum is a daily event in which the Development Team answers three questions to create a plan for the road ahead. The questions are as follows:

  • What did I do yesterday to help achieve the Sprint Goal?
  • What will I do today to help achieve the Sprint Goal?
  • Is there anything stopping me from meeting the Sprint Goal?
By having each member of the Development Team answer these questions, organization and work can be performed more efficiently, minimizing the need for other meetings and ensuring that what must be done is done and what prevents others from doing is eliminated before it becomes a problem.

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a framework for software and Web development. It is unique in that it breaks the development process down to small parts, called "sprints," that allow for the generation of a complete, potentially-shippable product in shorter times than other frameworks, which is then built upon to generate a finished product in an efficient manner.
